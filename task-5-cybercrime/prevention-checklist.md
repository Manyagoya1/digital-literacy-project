# Stay Safe Online – Student Checklist

### Account & Password Security

☐ Use strong, unique passwords for every account (mix of letters, numbers, symbols). Don’t reuse your Instagram password for banking—lazy and dangerous.

☐ Turn on 2-Factor Authentication (2FA) for email, social media, and banking apps.

### Device & App Safety

☐ Download apps only from official stores (Play Store/App Store). Random APKs = malware risk.

☐ Keep your phone and apps updated—updates fix security loopholes, not just bugs.

### Phishing & Scam Awareness

☐ Never click suspicious links in emails/SMS/WhatsApp (especially “urgent” messages about prizes, jobs, or bank issues).

☐ Verify sender identity before sharing OTPs, passwords, or personal info—no legit company asks for OTP.

### UPI & Financial Safety

☐ Never approve “collect requests” (payment requests) on UPI unless you’re 100% sure. Receiving money does NOT require approval—only sending does.

☐ Don’t share UPI PIN, OTP, or screen-share your phone during any “customer care” call. That’s the most common scam trick right now.

### Public Wi-Fi & Browsing

☐ Avoid logging into banking or sensitive accounts on public Wi-Fi (college, cafes). Use mobile data or a VPN.

☐ Always check for “https://” before entering passwords on websites.

### Social Media Discipline

☐ Keep profiles private and limit personal info (phone number, address, college details).

☐ Think before posting—once it’s online, you’ve lost control of it.

### Data Protection

☐ Regularly backup important files (notes, IDs, assignments) to cloud or external storage.

☐ Log out from shared computers—don’t assume the next person is honest.

## If Something Goes Wrong

Report immediately on the National Cyber Crime Portal: https://www.cybercrime.gov.in

Call the Cyber Crime Helpline: 1930 (especially for financial fraud—timing matters)
